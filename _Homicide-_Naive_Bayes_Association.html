Dealing With Odd Age Range

Assume minimum age is 0 and maximum age is 99 for the victim. I will assume minimum age for a perpetrator is three and the maximum age is 80. I do not want outliers influencing the results of the predictions. I have a hard time believing a victim happened to be 998 years old. Also hard to believe a perpetrator is less than 3 years old or older than 80 due to normal physical capabilities below three years old and above eighty years old.

newReport$Victim.Age <- ifelse(newReport$Victim.Age >= 0 & newReport$Victim.Age <= 99,
                     newReport$Victim.Age, NA)

newReport$Perpetrator.Age <- ifelse(newReport$Perpetrator.Age >= 3 & newReport$Perpetrator.Age <= 80,
                     newReport$Perpetrator.Age, NA)

Using a confidence interval of 80% is saying that the results of this sample, if repeated over and over, would match the results found in the actual population 80% of the time.

Recommendations Based on the Naive Bayes and Apriori Algorithm Models

Initially, I would use the Naive Bayes Model to predict a weapon that is most likely to be used in a murder. I would test this model out to see if the accuracy holds, however. I would not be suprised if the accuracy dropped the this model was predicting more than just observations for 2014. If had to limit the amount of observations used, I would still focus on more current years.

Using the Apriori Algorithm makes generating good, solid rules for the top influential predictors of weapon choice difficult. However, this model stills give a good picture into the relations involved in a homicide. The Apriori Algorithm appears to give weight to the victim’s age, the perpetrator’s age, the victim’s sex, and the victim’s race. This model seems to support the victim being more of a determinant to how and why a homicide occurs than does the perpetrator. Due to this, I believe it is very important to educate the population on how to better protect themselves. Those more likely to be victims need to be assured help is available before it is too late.
